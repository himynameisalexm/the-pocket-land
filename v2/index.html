The Craft

Specified over a lifetime.
Built without compromise.

Every detail in The Pocket Land was chosen to age — not wear out. Quartzite Mont Blanc benchtops. Perrin & Rowe bridge tap over a farmhouse sink. Sash windows with custom timber ember screens running the full length of the kitchen, opening to the paddock every morning.

It’s the kind of specification you recognise before you understand it — a coherence that runs from room to room without announcing itself. Chosen to age in place, not to impress at inspection.

The Land

Land that
earns its keep.

Set at 530 metres above sea level, on land formerly held by the Commonwealth Government as a radar installation, this property exists at this scale for one reason: it was never subdivided. Three hundred and sixty degrees of native bushland surrounds every boundary — completely untouched. Eight hectares, five cleared acres, fully fenced for animals or children.

Most acreage needs constant managing. This one doesn’t. What the list below describes isn’t a feature set — it’s a different relationship with the land.

14.6kW solar, 36 panels — 25.6kW battery storage
3 × 22,500L in-ground concrete tanks — includes bushfire reserve
Triple-filtered water & solar hot water system
200m private driveway — end of a quiet no-through road
Hand-placed rock walls sourced entirely from the property
10+ fruit trees — orange, lemon, mango, avocado, plum & more

The Setting

Fifteen minutes from Maleny.
The rest of the world
is closer than it looks.

Maleny is fifteen minutes down the road and worth every minute of it. A genuine hinterland town — weekly farmers market, independent restaurants, galleries, and a hardware store that's been there for forty years. Not a tourist town. A real one that the locals actually use.

Reesville itself sits in a protected pocket of the Sunshine Coast Hinterland, flanked by national park and bordered by properties of similar scale and intention. The house is positioned full north — rolling misty mornings and uninterrupted sunsets across the ranges, with wallabies in the paddock most evenings. The Sunshine Coast is under an hour; Brisbane under ninety minutes. Private without being remote.
